# Global HIV Trend Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This Power BI project analyzes global HIV trends, treatment coverage, mortality risk, and regional disparities. The dashboard provides an interactive view of HIV-related indicators across countries, continents, and years, helping identify patterns in disease burden and treatment access.

## Project Objectives

The analysis was developed to:

* Analyze global trends in new HIV infections and HIV-related deaths.
* Compare the HIV burden across continents and countries.
* Evaluate antiretroviral therapy (ART) treatment coverage.
* Examine the relationship between treatment coverage and mortality.
* Identify countries with high treatment access risk.
* Provide data-driven insights and strategic recommendations.

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Global HIV Trends

This page provides an overview of:

* New HIV infections.
* HIV-related deaths.
* People living with HIV.
* Treatment coverage.
* Mortality and survival rates.
* Trends in new infections and deaths over time.
* Geographic and regional differences in HIV burden.

### 2. Treatment Access and Mortality Risk Analysis

This page examines:

* Global treatment coverage.
* Countries with high and low treatment coverage.
* The relationship between new HIV infections and treatment access.
* ART coverage compared with mortality rates.
* Countries with the highest treatment access risk scores.

### 3. Key Insights and Strategic Recommendations

The final page summarizes major findings from the analysis and presents recommendations focused on expanding ART access, prioritizing high-risk countries, strengthening prevention and early diagnosis, improving data-driven monitoring, and developing region-specific intervention strategies.

## Key Insights

* The HIV burden is unevenly distributed across regions, with Africa carrying the largest burden of people living with HIV.
* Global treatment coverage remains uneven, with substantial treatment access gaps in many countries.
* Countries with higher numbers of new infections often face greater treatment access challenges.
* Higher ART coverage is associated with better health outcomes and lower HIV-related mortality in many countries.
* Countries with high treatment access risk scores require targeted interventions and increased healthcare investment.
